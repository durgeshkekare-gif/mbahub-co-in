What NAAC grade means for an online MBA

NAAC (National Assessment and Accreditation Council) grades reflect the underlying university's academic quality — faculty, research, infrastructure and governance. A+ and A++ are the top two grades. They signal that the awarding institution has been formally assessed as meeting rigorous quality standards. For employers and background verification, NAAC grade of the university matters more than whether the program is "online".

Is NAAC A++ always better than A+ for your career?

Not always. Symbiosis SSODL (A++ parent) is more nationally recognised than Amrita (A++) for most industries outside South India. Chandigarh University (A+) is often more recognised in North India than BVDU (A++, Maharashtra). NAAC grade matters — but the institutional brand within your industry and geography matters more.
